## Why this site exists

In many places, “Dhamma” is reduced to:

- rituals and beliefs
- or simple feel-good ideas

But that is not what the Buddha taught.

This site exists to present Dhamma
 **as it actually is — direct, demanding, and practical.**

------

## What you’ll find here

- Essays written in clear language
- Practical reflections on experience
- A way to look at life directly

This is not meant to comfort you, but to help you **see**.

------

## Approach to Dhamma

The Dhamma presented here is:

- Not based on belief
- Grounded in experience and responsibility

Dhamma means:

- Understanding why suffering is there
- Seeing what you are holding onto
- Recognizing that freedom is not in acquiring, but in letting go

------

## Who this is for

This site is not for everyone.

It is for those who:

- Are willing to look seriously at life
- Are not searching for easy answers
- Are ready to question themselves

------

## Final note

If you genuinely want to understand,
 this site may be useful.

If you only want comfort,
 it may not be.
